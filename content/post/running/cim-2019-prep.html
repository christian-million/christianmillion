In just a few weeks, on August 5th 2019, I will begin training for the 2019 California International Marathon (CIM). The marathon will be held on December 8th, 2019. I will be following Hal Higdon’s 18-week Intermediate 2 Marathon Training Program.

Currently, my goal time for this marathon is 3 hours and 30 minutes. Though, halfway through the training, I am going to reevaluate my goal time, in order to set realistic expectations. My previous marathon time was 03:58:15. Because my new goal is ~30 minute faster than my previous time, my training is going to have to be intense. Focus, heart, and dedication will be necessary for this batch of training.

Reflection on CIM 2018 Training

Last year, I had three goals for CIM:

  • Finish!

  • Finish under 04:00:00

  • Finish under 03:45:00

I felt very good about my 3:58:15 time, because I acheived 2/3 goals, I was only 13 minutes under my goal time, and because it was my first marathon.

The Positives

During last years training, there were a few key components to my training that I think helped get so close to my goal:

  • Patience and Understanding

  • Awareness during runs

  • Solid Weekday runs

  • Friends to run with and motivate (Columbia College Running Ramblers).

  • Support from loved ones.

I was patient with myself and focused on awareness during my runs. Because it was my first marathon, I cut myself a lot of slack when I felt the 18 mile training run was too tough. Areas of Improvement

Training was tough, but as always, I could have tried harder. In general, I could:

  • Dedicated Strength Training

  • Pre-run Warm-Ups

  • Post-run Recovery (Stretches / Foam Rolling / Meals)

  • Better Meal Planning

  • More sleep

  • Morning Runs (Instead of the Evening Runs)

  • Take ALL long runs seriously.

Before 2018, I had never trained physically for anything. I had loosely followed a couple of strength training programs, but those only lasted a few weeks and progress was fleeting. Running is the only exercise I had ever engaged in and enjoyed.

Planning for CIM 2019

With a little more planning this year, I can take out some of the cognitive costs of training. This year my planning will include methods to tackle the areas of improvement above.
